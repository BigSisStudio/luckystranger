Concept A — Moodboard

The
Lucky
One

Optimistic. Warm. Hand-painted. This direction leans hard into the lucky — the sense that something good is about to happen and this is exactly the kind of place where it does. Utilitarian bones, Midwest dive bar DNA, a logo that looks like it's been above the door for twenty years and belongs there. It doesn't try to be cool. It just is.

The tension

Script "Lucky" vs. block "STRANGER" — two words pulling in slightly different directions, held together.

The detail

The L-swash is the standout move. Connective tissue between letterforms that rewards a closer look.

The mark

Cat options range from silhouette to building illustration. Flexible — doesn't have to be chosen today.

What it opens

Warm enough to be approachable. Enough craft to earn trust. Matchbook already looks like it belongs.

Concept B — Moodboard

The
Stranger

Mid-century refined. Quietly knowing. This direction leans into the stranger — the person at the end of the bar you can't quite place but immediately trust. Intelligent, slightly unknowable, with a visual register that suggests something specific happened in this room in 1963 and no one's fully explained it since. Not mysterious for mystery's sake. Considered.

The mark to watch

The peeking cat eyes above the badge. Distinctive, noir, immediately memorable. That detail does a lot of work.

Built-in tagline

"Drinks · Decency · Discretion" is already living inside the badge. The logo and the voice arrived together.

Two moods in one system

Navy container = considered and cool. Red shield = authority with swagger. Worth discussing which to lead with.

What it opens

The most developed of the two systems. Sets up Lucky Stranger Disco (LSD) as a natural evolution of this register.
